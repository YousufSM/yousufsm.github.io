Business Process Optimization: Streamlining Order to Cash pipeline Resulting in Addition of $300M in Working Capital & Reduction of Throughput Time by 7 Days


Industry: Tech, B2B
Use Case: Business Process Optimization, Order 2 Cash Cycle Optimization, Process Mining

Situation: A billion-dollar tech client was facing hurdles in their order to cash processes, which were impacting high re-work, causing delivery delays, and leading to disputes. The organization operated on SAP system, yet the lack of process transparency was bottleneck to the business flow.

Task: The task was to perform a diagnostic project to scrutinize and optimize the client's business processes, understanding how the process flows right from creating a sales order to clearing the invoice. The objective was to create a blue print in front of CFO and CEO to show the board how the process happens in the org, what are the inefficiencies and what's the impact of that

Action: Performed process mining using Celonis to delve into the SAP system. Performing ETL to convert SAP data into process mining understandable format. Analyzed process flow right from "when a customer place an order" to the goods have been issued and customer "clear the billing". Analyzed and created dashboards discussing late-deliveries, un-billed orders, late-billing orders, manual processes, re-work, late bill payments, order blocks, payment term mismatches and disputes and analyzed these KPIs with it's impact on throughput time, automation, customer satisfaction and working capital flow.

Result: The optimization initiative led to a 70% reduction in order rejections, unlocked an additional $110M in working capital flow, and reduced overall flow by 7 days. It also brought down payment term mismatches by 20% and Days Sales Outstanding by 3 days.

Pioneering a National Agricultural Database: Connecting Farmers with Agricultural Lands



Industry: Government
Use Case: Algorithm Designing, PoC

Situation: The Central Government envisioned creating a distinctive national-level database that would amalgamate agricultural land data with farmer information. This initiative was one of it's kind as no other major country had ventured into such a project. The goal was to deliver a Proof of Concept (PoC) to the Prime Minister's Office, highlighting the viability and possible success rate of the project

Task: The task was to classify provinces (States) based on their existing data infrastructure, technology maturity, and database availability. Then for each category, design a PoCs to exhibit the efficacy of tagging lands with farmer data.

Action: Commenced by scrutinizing the infrastructure, technology maturity, and database readiness in each province. Following this, segregated each province into four segments based on the evaluations. A distinct PoC was conceptualized for each category, totalling four PoCs. Then, developing a detailed algorithm to map land record to farmer's databases based on multiple State and Central Gov databases, voter identification cards & phonetic name matching of farmer. The success ratio of tagging lands with farmers exhibited variation across the PoCs. In provinces characterized by high tech maturity and database accessibility, a success rate of 60% was attained, while in others, it hovered at 22%.

Result: The diverse success rates across different provinces imparted invaluable insights into the hurdles and potential remedies for this national-scale initiative. The case, along with the discoveries, was presented to the Prime Minister's Office. The case initiated a significant milestone in pioneering comprehensive agricultural database with the potential to revolutionize the nation's agricultural framework.

Fast-Track Financing: Harnessing Machine Learning for Speedy and Transparent Credit Evaluations


Industry: Banking
Use Case: Credit Risk Modelling

Situation: An India-based Non-Banking Financial Company (NBFC) had a robust decision-making mechanism for loan disbursement, yet it took around 5 days to process a single application. The lengthy decision time was seen as a bottleneck in enhancing their service efficiency.

Task: The task was to create a machine learning (ML) model to expedite the decision-making process by providing a risk-based scoring to applicants, aiming to cut down the decision time and make the process more transparent.

Action: Developed a Credit Risk ML model using R, which leveraged bureau, financial, and application data to score applicants. The model bucketed applications based on predicted risk, also providing reason codes for the scores to maintain transparency in decision-making. Result: The implementation of the ML model successfully reduced the decision time from 5 days to 3 days, cutting down 30% of the manual screening workload. Industry: Telecommunication
Use Case: Location prioritization, AI Based Business Strategy

Situation: Vodafone UK was poised to invest in the promising arena of 5G technology. However, with multiple locations vying for investment, the challenge was to determine which locations would be the most strategic and offer the best return on investment.

Task: The task was to design a robust ranking methodology that could prioritize these locations effectively for 5G investments. This methodology would ensure that Vodafone UK's investments were strategically placed to maximize impact and profitability.

Action: Multiple GeoClustering algorithms were stacked together to divide geographies into relatable and cohesive areas. Result: The GeoClustering ML approach provided Vodafone UK with a clear and actionable roadmap for their 5G investments. Locations were not just chosen based on their inherent potential, but on a comprehensive strategy that took into account various factors to ensure optimal investment placement. This strategic initiative streamlined Vodafone UK's investment process, ensuring they were positioned to lead in the 5G revolution.
